Power of AI in detecting, diagnosing and delivering

     Artificial intelligence plays a vital role in modern diagnostics. It analyzes complex medical data such as medical images including X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans, electronic health records, genetic information, and patient-reported symptoms. These AI systems assist healthcare professionals in detecting, monitoring, and diagnosing diseases. The primary goal is not to replace clinicians but to support them by improving diagnostic accuracy, minimizing human error, and enhancing clinical workflows for more efficient and informed patient care.

     Traditional diagnosis relies heavily on physician expertise, manual data review, and clinical experience. AI processes large volumes of data quickly and identifies subtle patterns not easily seen by humans.

How AI Is Changing the Medical Field

     In diagnostics and imaging, AI algorithms are used to analyze X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs, detecting diseases like cancer and neurological disorders with remarkable accuracy and speed. For instance, AI models have achieved diagnostic accuracies over 90% for certain cancers and viral infections, and can identify abnormalities in just minutes, enabling faster and more reliable diagnoses (Rahman et al., 2024)

What is the role of AI in Diagnosing

     Machine Learning enables computers to learn from vast datasets and identify patterns to make predictions or recommendations. Used widely for diagnostics, risk prediction, and treatment planning (Barth, 2024). Deep Learning utilizes neural networks with multiple layers, particularly good for analyzing medical images like X-ray and MRIs. Natural language processing (NLP) allows AI to extract information from medical records, automate documentation, and power virtual assistants or chatbots to support patients.

Real World Application

     Zebra Medical Vision uses deep learning and computer vision to analyze radiology images, assisting radiologists in detecting conditions like cancer, cardiovascular issues, and liver diseases. Their AI improves diagnostic accuracy by 25%, reduces misdiagnoses, and speeds up report turnaround times, enhancing overall efficiency and patient care (Filipsson, 2025).
