AI in Environment

Overview Sub Topic Sub Topic Sub Topic Sub Topic Sub Topic Sub Topic Sub Topic Sub Topic Future of AI

     AI entered the field of environment since manual-based monitoring requires large resources and manpower. Experts across industries are using artificial intelligence as a tool to help clean up the planet, reduce plastic pollution, lower global emissions, and create greener tech (Howell, 2024). By using AI, it boosts efficiency and allows monitoring of the environment real-time, causing faster actions to prevent environmental disasters, contributing to a more sustainable future.

     Advancement of technology causing AI to gain more attention than ever. Advanced tools including deforestation detection, locating and identifying plastic debris in the ocean, and many more. AI is gaining attention right now because of its uses in various environment fields, such as:

  • Greenhouse Gasses Monitoring
  • Renewable Energy Optimization
  • Water Usage Optimization
  • Climate Change Prediction and Impact
  • Flood Risk Prediction
  • Crop Disease Detection
  • Smart Agriculture

      These fields are the ones who benefited the most in using AI when it comes to the environment. Advancement of tools with the use of AI makes the work easier and much more efficient.

How AI Is Changing the Environmental Field

           In global emission, AI is used to make supply more efficient, improving building efficiency, assessing transportation methods, and optimizing energy systems. It can identify opportunities for transportation routes optimization, reduce packaging waste, and minimize inventory levels. AI can also help businesses monitor and control building systems, assess transportation methods, and optimize energy systems, enabling transitions from fossil fuels to cleaner, renewable energy.

     AI enhances accuracy, speed and cost effectiveness in Environment. AI models can outperform humans especially when it comes to environmental monitoring. For environmental monitoring, AI can process large volumes of satellite imagery to assess land-use changes and deforestation whilst also assessing data from environmental sensors to evaluate pollution levels (Ebbah, 2024). This can be useful in current times because the earth is continuing to warm due to the effects of greenhouse gas emissions. It is also much cheaper than using a manual method of monitoring the environment, because it automates data collection and analysis, reducing the need for human labor and minimizing human error.

     Traditional environmental monitoring techniques often involve manual sample collection and laboratory analysis, which can be time-consuming and provide data with a lag (Environmental Monitoring: Techniques | StudySmarter, n.d.). Modern techniques, on the other hand, use technology to provide continuous and real-time monitoring, allowing for immediate insights into environmental conditions. With the use of AI, it further enhances the operation of modern techniques of environment monitoring. AI provides many benefits when it comes to the environment.

Technologies Behind the AI

      There are different types of AI that are used in the environmental sector, each of them has their own advantages. For instance, AI models such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are used for image-based environmental monitoring tasks like detecting deforestation and identifying wildlife (Bidari and Chickerur, 2024). Another type of AI that is used in the environment sector is Recurrent Neural Networks. Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are utilized for time series forecasting, such as predicting flood events based on historical rainfall data (Chen et al., 2020).

     Environmental applications of AI technologies are enabled by the integration of data collection equipment, machine learning platforms, cloud infrastructure, and geographic information systems. Remote sensing satellites, unmanned aerial vehicles, IoT sensors, and environmental databases supply the raw data for analysis.

Where It’s Being Used Now

            Google is using AI to improve global flood forecasting, offering more accurate river flood predictions up to seven days in advance (Dilmegani, 2025). Through its Flood Hub platform, which provides free real-time forecasts, the initiative has expanded to 80 countries, reaching 460 million people, including those in vulnerable communities (Dilmegani, 2025). For monitoring forest fires, Dryad Networks has installed over 400 "electronic noses" in the Eberswalde forest in Brandenburg, a region heavily impacted by wildfires. These sensors can detect gasses in the early stages of a fire and monitor temperature, humidity, and air pressure. And lastly, for fighting air pollution. The IQAir program provides a ranking that displays which towns have the highest air pollution in real time. The Plume Labs app displays detailed maps of areas with the highest pollution levels. The program also predicts what the air quality will be like every hour, as levels can fluctuate dramatically during the day.

      Companies such as Google, Dryad Networks, Plume Labs, and IQAir are leading the way in terms of environmental monitoring using AI, utilizing their technologies to safeguard the planet and encourage a more sustainable future.

      In developed countries such as the United States, Japan, and select areas in Europe, AI-based solutions are used within grand-scale systems to provide real-time flood prediction, smart energy systems, and self-driven pollution sensing. By comparison, the emerging adoption of Africa, as well as Southeast Asia, have relatively less access to reliable data, computational infrastructure, and proficient AI practitioners. Consequently, their application of AI is more likely to be on a small-scale, grant-funded pilot project like basic weather forecasting or waste management programs.
